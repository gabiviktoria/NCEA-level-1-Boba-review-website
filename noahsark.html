Review

The design of Noah’s Ark stores are all really nice and tidy, with simple decorations. It took 20 minutes to get my drink because of a long waiting line. My order was a warm Matcha milk tea with pearls and 25% sugar which was $8.50. This was the most expensive drink out af all the drinks on this boba blog. This is because I had to get a large size since they only have that option.

It’s obvious that environmentally, Noah’s Ark has the best packaging out of all the bubble tea shops I went to. The cups and seal were recyclable, not plastic, and the straw was paper. Thie paper straws didn't get soggy over time and th cup kept my drink warm without burning my hand.

Since I got this as a warm drink I thought that it would give the matcha powder and creamer a better chance to dissolve but it didn’t. The matcha was terrible, it tasted like powdery water instead of sweet, creamy matcha. I would even prefer the Gong Cha japanese matcha milk tea over this.

The pearls redeemed the fact that the milk tea was terrible. The size allowed them to not get stuck in my straw and they were perfectly cooked so the pearls didn’t clump together.
